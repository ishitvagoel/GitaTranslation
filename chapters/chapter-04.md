# Chapter 4 — Jñānakarmasaṃnyāsayogaḥ (ज्ञानकर्मसंन्यासयोगः)

**The Yoga of Knowledge and the Renunciation of Action** · 42 verses

---

### 4.1

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
इमं विवस्वते योगं प्रोक्तवानहमव्ययम् ।
विवस्वान्मनवे प्राह मनुरिक्ष्वाकवेऽब्रवीत् ॥ १ ॥

*śrībhagavān uvāca |*
*imaṃ vivasvate yogaṃ proktavān aham avyayam |*
*vivasvān manave prāha manur ikṣvākave 'bravīt || 1 ||*

The Blessed Lord said:
I taught this imperishable yoga to Vivasvān; Vivasvān declared it to Manu, and Manu spoke it to Ikṣvāku.

> **Note:** *avyayam* — imperishable or eternal. Kṛṣṇa establishes the antiquity and continuity of this knowledge, tracing its lineage through solar and royal progenitors.

---

### 4.2

एवं परम्पराप्राप्तमिमं राजर्षयो विदुः ।
स कालेनेह महता योगो नष्टः परन्तप ॥ २ ॥

*evaṃ paramparāpraptam imaṃ rājarṣayo viduḥ |*
*sa kāleneha mahatā yogo naṣṭaḥ parantapa || 2 ||*

Thus received through succession, the royal sages knew this. Through the long passage of time, that yoga was lost here, O scorcher of foes.

---

### 4.3

स एवायं मया तेऽद्य योगः प्रोक्तः पुरातनः ।
भक्तोऽसि मे सखा चेति रहस्यं ह्येतदुत्तमम् ॥ ३ ॥

*sa evāyaṃ mayā te 'dya yogaḥ proktaḥ purātanaḥ |*
*bhakto 'si me sakhā ceti rahasyaṃ hy etad uttamam || 3 ||*

That same ancient yoga is today declared by Me to you, for you are My devotee and My friend; indeed, this is the supreme secret.

---

### 4.4

अर्जुन उवाच ।
अपरं भवतो जन्म परं जन्म विवस्वतः ।
कथमेतद्विजानीयां त्वमादौ प्रोक्तवानिति ॥ ४ ॥

*arjuna uvāca |*
*aparaṃ bhavato janma paraṃ janma vivasvataḥ |*
*katham etad vijānīyāṃ tvam ādau proktavān iti || 4 ||*

Arjuna said:
Your birth is recent, and the birth of Vivasvān was ancient. How should I understand this — that You taught it in the beginning?

---

### 4.5

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
बहूनी मे व्यतीतानि जन्मानि तव चार्जुन ।
तान्यहं वेद सर्वाणि न त्वं वेत्थ परन्तप ॥ ५ ॥

*śrībhagavān uvāca |*
*bahūni me vyatītāni janmāni tava cārjuna |*
*tāny ahaṃ veda sarvāṇi na tvaṃ vettha parantapa || 5 ||*

The Blessed Lord said:
Many births of Mine have passed, and yours also, Arjuna. I know them all, but you do not know them, O scorcher of foes.

---

### 4.6

अजोऽपि सन्नव्ययात्मा भूतानामीश्वरोऽपि सन् ।
प्रकृतिं स्वामधिष्ठाय सम्भवाम्यात्ममायया ॥ ६ ॥

*ajo 'pi sann avyayātmā bhūtānām īśvaro 'pi san |*
*prakṛtiṃ svām adhiṣṭhāya sambhavāmy ātmamāyayā || 6 ||*

Although being unborn and of imperishable nature, and although being the Lord of all beings, governing My own nature (*prakṛti*), I manifest by My own power (*māyā*).

> **Note:** *ātmamāyayā* — by My own power or creative illusion. Kṛṣṇa explains the mechanism of divine manifestation: it is not a "birth" forced by *guṇas* (as described in 3.5), but a self-willed governance of *prakṛti*.

---

### 4.7

यदा यदा हि धर्मस्य ग्लानिर्भवति भारत ।
अभ्युत्थानमधर्मस्य तदात्मानं सृजाम्यहम् ॥ ७ ॥

*yadā yadā hi dharmasya glānir bhavati bhārata |*
*abhyutthānam adharmasya tadātmānaṃ sṛjāmy aham || 7 ||*

Whenever there is a decline of righteousness (*dharma*), O Bhārata, and a rise of unrighteousness, then I manifest Myself.

---

### 4.8

परित्राणाय साधूनां विनाशाय च दुष्कृताम् ।
धर्मसंस्थापनार्थाय सम्भवामि युगे युगे ॥ ८ ॥

*paritrāṇāya sādhūnāṃ vināśāya ca duṣkṛtām |*
*dharmasaṃsthāpanārthāya sambhavāmi yuge yuge || 8 ||*

For the protection of the virtuous and for the destruction of evil-doers, for the purpose of establishing righteousness, I manifest age after age.

---

### 4.9

जन्म कर्म च मे दिव्यमेवं यो वेत्ति तत्त्वतः ।
त्यक्त्वा देहं पुनर्जन्म नैति मामेति सोऽर्जुन ॥ ९ ॥

*janma karma ca me divyam evaṃ yo vetti tattvataḥ |*
*tyaktvā dehaṃ punarjanma naiti mām eti so 'rjuna || 9 ||*

One who thus knows in truth My divine birth and action, having abandoned the body, does not undergo rebirth; he comes to Me, Arjuna.

---

### 4.10

वीतरागभयक्रोधा मन्मया मामुपाश्रिताः ।
बहवो ज्ञानतपसा पूता मद्भावमागताः ॥ १० ॥

*vītarāgabhayakrodhā manmayā mām upāśritāḥ |*
*bahavo jñānatapasā pūtā madbhāvam āgatāḥ || 10 ||*

Freed from attachment, fear, and anger, absorbed in Me, taking refuge in Me, many, purified by the penance of knowledge, have attained My state.

> **Note:** *jñānatapasā* — the penance or heat of knowledge. Purification is achieved here not through physical austerity but through the transformative power of the knowledge just described.

---

### 4.11

ये यथा मां प्रपद्यन्ते तांस्तथैव भजाम्यहम् ।
मम वर्त्मानुवर्तन्ते मनुष्याः पार्थ सर्वशः ॥ ११ ॥

*ye yathā māṃ prapadyante tāṃs tathaiva bhajāmy aham |*
*mama vartmānuvartante manuṣyāḥ pārtha sarvaśaḥ || 11 ||*

In whatever way people approach Me, in that same way I reciprocate with them. Men follow My path in every way, Pārtha.

> **Note:** Kṛṣṇa establishes the principle of divine reciprocity. The "path" (*vartma*) is universal; all seek the same source, but through diverse modes of approach.

---

### 4.12

काङ्क्षन्तः कर्मणां सिद्धिं यजन्त इह देवताः ।
क्षिप्रं हि मानुषे लोके सिद्धिर्भवति कर्मजा ॥ १२ ॥

*kāṅkṣantaḥ karmaṇāṃ siddhiṃ yajanta iha devatāḥ |*
*kṣipraṃ hi mānuṣe loke siddhir bhavati karmajā || 12 ||*

Desiring the success of actions, people here worship the gods; for in the human world, success born of action is quickly attained.

---

### 4.13

चातुर्वर्ण्यं मया सृष्टं गुणकर्मविभागशः ।
तस्य कर्तारमपि मां विद्ध्यकर्तारमव्ययम् ॥ १३ ॥

*cāturvarṇyaṃ mayā sṛṣṭaṃ guṇakarmavibhāgaśaḥ |*
*tasya kartāram api māṃ viddhy akartāram avyayam || 13 ||*

The fourfold order was created by Me according to the classification of qualities (*guṇas*) and actions (*karma*). Although I am its creator, know Me to be the non-doer and immutable.

> **Note:** *guṇakarmavibhāgaśaḥ* — Kṛṣṇa defines the social order not by birth, but by the psychological constituents (*guṇas*) and the resulting functional roles (*karma*). He remains the "non-doer" despite being the source.

---

### 4.14

न मां कर्माणि लिम्पन्ति न मे कर्मफले स्पृहा ।
इति मां योऽभिजानाति कर्मभिर्न स बध्यते ॥ १४ ॥

*na māṃ karmāṇi limpanti na me karma-phale spṛhā |*
*iti māṃ yo 'bhijānāti karmabhir na sa badhyate || 14 ||*

Actions do not taint Me, nor have I any desire for the fruit of actions. He who knows Me thus is not bound by actions.

---

### 4.15

एवं ज्ञात्वा कृतं कर्म पूर्वैरपि मुमुक्षुभिः ।
कुरु कर्मैव तस्मात्त्वं पूर्वैः पूर्वतरं कृतम् ॥ १५ ॥

*evaṃ jñātvā kṛtaṃ karma pūrvair api mumukṣubhiḥ |*
*kuru karmaiva tasmāt tvaṃ pūrvaiḥ pūrvataraṃ kṛtam || 15 ||*

Knowing this, action was performed by the ancients who sought liberation. Therefore, perform action as the ancients did in former times.

---

### 4.16

किं कर्म किमकर्मेति कवयोऽप्यत्र मोहिताः ।
तत्ते कर्म प्रवक्ष्यामि यज्ज्ञात्वा मोक्ष्यसेऽशुभात् ॥ १६ ॥

*kiṃ karma kim akarmeti kavayo 'py atra mohitāḥ |*
*tat te karma pravakṣyāmi yaj jñātvā mokṣyase 'śubhāt || 16 ||*

"What is action? What is inaction?" Even the wise are bewildered in this matter. I shall explain that action to you, knowing which you will be liberated from evil.

---

### 4.17

कर्मणो ह्यपि बोद्धव्यं बोद्धव्यं च विकर्मणः ।
अकर्मणश्च बोद्धव्यं गहना कर्मणो गतिः ॥ १७ ॥

*karmaṇo hy api boddhavyaṃ boddhavyaṃ ca vikarmaṇaḥ |*
*akarmaṇaś ca boddhavyaṃ gahanā karmaṇo gatiḥ || 17 ||*

One must understand the nature of action, the nature of forbidden action (*vikarma*), and the nature of inaction (*akarma*). Profound is the path of action.

---

### 4.18

कर्मण्यकर्म यः पश्येदकर्मणि च कर्म यः ।
स बुद्धिमान्मनुष्येषु स युक्तः कृत्स्नकर्मकृत् ॥ १८ ॥

*karmaṇy akarma yaḥ paśyed akarmaṇi ca karma yaḥ |*
*sa buddhimān manuṣyeṣu sa yuktaḥ kṛtsnakarmakṛt || 18 ||*

He who sees inaction in action, and action in inaction — he is intelligent among men; he is disciplined (*yukta*) and a performer of all actions.

> **Note:** The central paradox of Karma Yoga. Inaction in action refers to the absence of egoic agency during performance; action in inaction refers to the subtle mental activity that persists even during physical stillness.

---

### 4.19

यस्य सर्वे समारम्भाः कामसङ्कल्पवर्जिताः ।
ज्ञानाग्निदग्धकर्माणं तमाहुः पण्डितं बुधाः ॥ १९ ॥

*yasya sarve samārambhāḥ kāmasaṅkalpavarjitāḥ |*
*jñānāgni-dagdha-karmāṇaṃ tam āhuḥ paṇḍitaṃ budhāḥ || 19 ||*

One whose every undertaking is free from desire and [selfish] purpose (*saṅkalpa*), and whose actions are burnt by the fire of knowledge — him the wise call a sage.

> **Note:** *jñānāgni-dagdha-karmāṇaṃ* — "one whose actions are burnt by the fire of knowledge." The causal power of *karma* to bind the individual is neutralized by the realization of non-agency.

---

### 4.20

त्यक्त्वा कर्मफलासङ्गं नित्यतृप्तो निराश्रयः ।
कर्मण्यभिप्रवृत्तोऽपि नैव किञ्चित्करोति सः ॥ २० ॥

*tyaktvā karma-phalāsaṅgaṃ nitya-tṛpto nirāśrayaḥ |*
*karmaṇy abhipravṛtto 'pi naiva kiñcit karoti saḥ || 20 ||*

Abandoning attachment to the fruit of action, ever satisfied and independent, even though fully engaged in action, he indeed does nothing at all.

---

### 4.21

निराशीर्यतचित्तात्मा त्यक्तसर्वपरिग्रहः ।
शारीरं केवलं कर्म कुर्वन्नाप्नोति किल्बिषम् ॥ २१ ॥

*nirāśīr yata-cittātmā tyakta-sarva-parigrahaḥ |*
*śārīraṃ kevalaṃ karma kurvan nāpnoti kilbiṣam || 21 ||*

Free from expectations, with mind and self controlled, having abandoned all possessiveness, performing action merely for the sake of the body, he incurs no stain.

---

### 4.22

यदृच्छालाभसन्तुष्टो द्वन्द्वातीतो विमत्सरः ।
समः सिद्धावसिद्धौ च कृत्वापि न निबध्यते ॥ २२ ॥

*yadṛcchālābhasantuṣṭo dvandvātīto vimatsaraḥ |*
*samaḥ siddhāv asiddhau ca kṛtvāpi na nibadhyate || 22 ||*

Satisfied with whatever gain comes by chance, rising above dualities, free from envy, equanimous in success and failure — he is not bound even though he performs actions.

---

### 4.23

गतसङ्गस्य मुक्तस्य ज्ञानावस्थितचेतसः ।
यज्ञायाचरतः कर्म समग्रं प्रविलीयते ॥ २३ ॥

*gatasaṅgasya muktasya jñānavasthitacetasaḥ |*
*yajñāyācarataḥ karma samagraṃ pravilīyate || 23 ||*

For one who is devoid of attachment, who is liberated, whose mind is established in knowledge, and who performs action for the sake of sacrifice (*yajña*), action dissolves entirely.

---

### 4.24

ब्रह्मार्पणं ब्रह्म हविर्ब्रह्माग्नौ ब्रह्मणा हुतम् ।
ब्रह्मैव तेन गन्तव्यं ब्रह्मकर्मसमाधिना ॥ २४ ॥

*brahmārpaṇaṃ brahma havir brahmāgnau brahmaṇā hutam |*
*brahmaiva tena gantavyaṃ brahmakarmasamādhinā || 24 ||*

Brahman is the offering; Brahman is the oblation; by Brahman is the oblation poured into the fire of Brahman. Brahman alone is to be reached by him who is absorbed in action as Brahman.

> **Note:** Kṛṣṇa redefines *yajña* (sacrifice). It is no longer an external ritual but a total cognitive absorption where the actor, the action, and the objects are all realized as the Absolute (*Brahman*).

---

### 4.25

दैवमेवापरे यज्ञं योगिनः पर्युपासते ।
ब्रह्माग्नावपरे यज्ञं यज्ञेनैवोपजुह्वति ॥ २५ ॥

*daivam evāpare yajñaṃ yoginaḥ paryupāsate |*
*brahmāgnāv apare yajñaṃ yajñenaivopajuhvati || 25 ||*

Some yogis perfectly worship the sacrifice to the gods; others offer the sacrifice by the sacrifice itself in the fire of Brahman.

---

### 4.26

श्रोत्रादीनीन्द्रियाण्यन्ये संयमाग्निषु जुह्वति ।
शब्दादीन्विषयानन्य इन्द्रियाग्निषु जुह्वति ॥ २६ ॥

*śrotrādīnīndriyāṇy anye saṃyamāgniṣu juhvati |*
*śabdādīn viṣayān anya indriyāgniṣu juhvati || 26 ||*

Others offer hearing and other senses in the fires of restraint; still others offer sound and other objects in the fires of the senses.

> **Note:** Kṛṣṇa categorizes various forms of "internal sacrifice." Here, the objects of sacrifice are the cognitive faculties and their sensory inputs, kindled through the fire of discipline.

---

### 4.27

सर्वाणीन्द्रियकर्माणि प्राणकर्माणि चापरे ।
आत्मसंयमयोगाग्नौ जुह्वति ज्ञानदीपिते ॥ २७ ॥

*sarvāṇīndriyakarmāṇi prāṇakarmāṇi cāpare |*
*ātmasaṃyamayogāgnau juhvati jñānadīpite || 27 ||*

Others offer all functions of the senses and functions of the life-breath (*prāṇa*) in the fire of the yoga of self-restraint, kindled by knowledge.

---

### 4.28

द्रव्ययज्ञास्तपोयज्ञा योगयज्ञास्तथापरे ।
स्वाध्यायज्ञानयज्ञाश्च यतयः संशितव्रताः ॥ २८ ॥

*dravyayajñās tapoyajñā yogayajñās tathāpare |*
*svādhyāyajñānayajñāś ca yatayaḥ saṃśitavratāḥ || 28 ||*

There are those who sacrifice material possessions, those who sacrifice through austerity, and those whose sacrifice is the practice of yoga; likewise, striving men of strict vows perform the sacrifice of study and knowledge.

---

### 4.29

अपाने जुह्वति प्राणं प्राणेऽपानं तथापरे ।
प्रणापानगती रुद्ध्वा प्राणायामपरायणाः ॥ २९ ॥

*apāne juhvati prāṇaṃ prāṇe 'pānaṃ tathāpare |*
*prāṇāpānagatī ruddhvā prāṇāyāmaparāyaṇāḥ || 29 ||*

Others, dedicated to breath-control (*prāṇāyāma*), offer the incoming breath into the outgoing, and the outgoing into the incoming, restraining the flow of both breaths.

---

### 4.30

अपरे नियताहाराः प्राणान्प्राणेषु जुह्वति ।
सर्वेऽप्येते यज्ञविदो यज्ञक्षपितकल्मषाः ॥ ३० ॥

*apare niyatāhārāḥ prāṇān prāṇeṣu juhvati |*
*sarve 'py ete yajñavido yajñakṣapitakalmaṣāḥ || 30 ||*

Others, restricting their food, offer their life-breaths into the life-breaths. All these are knowers of sacrifice, their stains destroyed by sacrifice.

---

### 4.31

यज्ञशिष्टामृतभुजो यान्ति ब्रह्म सनातनम् ।
नायं लोकोऽस्त्ययज्ञस्य कुतोऽन्यः कुरुसत्तम ॥ ३१ ॥

*yajñaśiṣṭāmṛtabhujo yānti brahma sanātanam |*
*nāyaṃ loko 'sty ayajñasya kuto 'nyaḥ kurusattama || 31 ||*

Eating the nectar of the remnants of sacrifice, they go to the eternal Brahman. This world is not for one who does not sacrifice; how then can the other [world] be, O best of the Kurus?

---

### 4.32

एवं बहुविधा यज्ञा वितता ब्रह्मणो मुखे ।
कर्मजान्विद्धि तान्सर्वान्वेवं ज्ञात्वा विमोक्ष्यसे ॥ ३२ ॥

*evaṃ bahuvidhā yajñā vitatā brahmaṇo mukhe |*
*karmajān viddhi tān sarvān evaṃ jñātvā vimokṣyase || 32 ||*

Thus manifold sacrifices are spread out in the mouth of Brahman (the Veda). Know them all to be born of action; knowing this, you shall be liberated.

---

### 4.33

श्रेयान्द्रव्यमयाद्यज्ञाज्ज्ञानयज्ञः परन्तप ।
सर्वं कर्माखिलं पार्थ ज्ञाने परिसमाप्यते ॥ ३३ ॥

*śreyān dravyamayād yajñāj jñānayajñaḥ parantapa |*
*sarvaṃ karmākhilaṃ pārtha jñāne parisamāpyate || 33 ||*

Superior to the sacrifice of material objects is the sacrifice of knowledge, O scorcher of foes. All action in its entirety, Pārtha, culminates in knowledge.

---

### 4.34

तद्विद्धि प्रणिपातेन परिप्रश्नेन सेवया ।
उपदेक्ष्यन्ति ते ज्ञानं ज्ञानिनस्तत्त्वदर्शिनः ॥ ३४ ॥

*tadviddhi praṇipātena paripraśnena sevayā |*
*upadekṣyanti te jñānaṃ jñāninastattvadarśinaḥ || 34 ||*

Know that [knowledge] by prostrating, by questioning, and by service. The wise, the seers of truth, will instruct you in knowledge.

> **Note:** Kṛṣṇa emphasizes the pedagogical structure of traditional knowledge transmission: humility (*praṇipāta*), critical inquiry (*paripraśna*), and experiential service (*sevā*).

---

### 4.35

यज्ज्ञात्वा न पुनर्मोहमेवं यास्यसि पाण्डव ।
येन भूतान्यशेषेण द्रक्ष्यस्यात्मन्यथो मयि ॥ ३५ ॥

*yaj jñātvā na punar moham evaṃ yāsyasi pāṇḍava |*
*yena bhūtāny aśeṣeṇa drakṣyasy ātmany atho mayi || 35 ||*

Knowing which, you will not again fall into delusion, O son of Pāṇḍu; by this, you will see all beings without exception in the self, and then in Me.

---

### 4.36

अपि चेदसि पापेभ्यः सर्वेभ्यः पापकृत्तमः ।
सर्वं ज्ञानप्लवेनैव वृजिनं सन्तरिष्यसि ॥ ३६ ॥

*api ced asi pāpebhyaḥ sarvebhyaḥ pāpakṛttamaḥ |*
*sarvaṃ jñānaplavenaiva vṛjinaṃ santariṣyasi || 36 ||*

Even if you were the greatest of all evil-doers, you shall cross over all evil by the boat of knowledge alone.

---

### 4.37

यथैधांसि समिद्धोऽग्निर्भस्मसात्कुरुतेऽर्जुन ।
ज्ञानाग्निः सर्वकर्माणि भस्मसात्कुरुते तथा ॥ ३७ ॥

*yathaidhāṃsi samiddho 'gnir bhasmasāt kurute 'rjuna |*
*jñānāgniḥ sarvakarmāṇi bhasmasāt kurute tathā || 37 ||*

As a well-kindled fire turns fuel to ashes, Arjuna, so does the fire of knowledge turn all actions to ashes.

---

### 4.38

न हि ज्ञानेन सदृशं पवित्रमिह विद्यते ।
तत्स्वयं योगसंसिद्धः कालेनात्मनि विन्दति ॥ ३८ ॥

*na hi jñānena sadṛśaṃ pavitram iha vidyate |*
*tat svayaṃ yogasaṃsiddhaḥ kālenātmani vindati || 38 ||*

Indeed, there is no purifier in this world equal to knowledge. One who is perfected in yoga finds that [knowledge] within the self in due time.

---

### 4.39

श्रद्धावाँल्लभते ज्ञानं तत्परः संयतेन्द्रियः ।
ज्ञानं लब्ध्वा परां शान्तिमचिरेणाधिगच्छति ॥ ३९ ॥

*śraddhāvāḻ labhate jñānaṃ tatparaḥ saṃyatendriyaḥ |*
*jñānaṃ labdhvā parāṃ śāntim acireṇādhigacchati || 39 ||*

A man of faith attains knowledge, being devoted to it and having his senses controlled. Having attained knowledge, he reaches supreme peace without delay.

---

### 4.40

अज्ञश्चाश्रद्दधानश्च संशयात्मा विनश्यति ।
नायं लोकोऽस्ति न परो न सुखं संशयात्मनः ॥ ४० ॥

*ajñaś cāśraddadhānaś ca saṁśayātmā vinaśyati |*
*nāyaṃ loko 'sti na paro na sukhaṃ saṁśayātmanaḥ || 40 ||*

The ignorant, the faithless, and the doubting soul are ruined. Neither this world nor the next, nor happiness, exists for the doubting soul.

---

### 4.41

योगसंन्यस्तकर्माणं ज्ञानसंछिन्नसंशयम् ।
आत्मवन्तं न कर्माणि निबध्नन्ति धनञ्जय ॥ ४१ ॥

*yogasaṃnyastakarmāṇaṃ jñānasaṃchinnasaṃśayam |*
*ātmavantaṃ na karmāṇi nibadhnanti dhanañjaya || 41 ||*

Actions do not bind him who has renounced actions through yoga, whose doubts are cut away by knowledge, and who is self-possessed, O winner of wealth.

---

### 4.42

तस्मादज्ञानसंभूतं हृत्स्थं ज्ञानासिनात्मनः ।
छित्त्वैनं संशयं योगमातिष्ठोत्तिष्ठ भारत ॥ ४२ ॥

*tasmād ajñānasaṃbhūtaṃ hṛtsthaṃ jñānāsinātmanaḥ |*
*chittvainaṃ saṃśayaṃ yogam ātiṣṭhottiṣṭha bhārata || 42 ||*

Therefore, with the sword of knowledge, cutting this doubt born of ignorance situated in your heart, establish yourself in yoga and arise, O descendant of Bharata!
