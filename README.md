# Bhagavad Gita — A First-Principles English Translation

A fresh English translation of the complete Bhagavad Gita (18 chapters, ~700 verses), translated directly from Sanskrit using the BORI (Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute) Critical Edition of the Mahabharata as the primary source text.

## Methodology

This translation was produced from first principles. The sole inputs are:

- The Sanskrit text of the BORI Critical Edition
- Pāṇinian grammar
- Established Sanskrit lexicons (Monier-Williams, Apte)
- The internal context of the Gita itself

No existing English translation was referenced, echoed, or aligned with during the translation process.

## Translation Principles

- **Context-sensitive word choice**: Key Sanskrit terms (*dharma*, *yoga*, *ātman*, *brahman*, *prakṛti*, *karma*, *buddhi*, etc.) are not given a single fixed English equivalent. Each occurrence is translated based on its immediate verse context and surrounding passage context.
- **No imported theological frameworks**: The translation does not read the text through any later interpretive tradition (Advaita, Dvaita, Viśiṣṭādvaita, ISKCON devotional theology, Western philosophical categories).
- **Philosophical density preserved**: Abstract verses remain abstract. The text is not simplified or devotionalized.
- **No archaisms**: No "thou," "verily," or "hath" unless the Sanskrit register genuinely demands formality.
- **Specific terms respected**: Terms like *Bhagavān*, *Īśvara*, *Puruṣottama* are translated or transliterated on their own merits, not replaced with generic terms like "God" or "Lord."
- **Gender-neutral where appropriate**: Gendered language is not introduced where the Sanskrit is gender-neutral.
- **Internal tensions preserved**: If the Gita contains philosophical tensions, they are left to stand rather than harmonized.

## Transliteration

All transliteration follows the IAST (International Alphabet of Sanskrit Transliteration) standard.
