# Translation Methodology

## Source Text

This translation uses the Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute (BORI) Critical Edition of the Mahābhārata as its primary Sanskrit source. The BORI edition represents the most rigorously reconstructed text of the Mahābhārata, produced through systematic comparison of manuscripts across all major recensions and stripped of later interpolations. Where the BORI edition records textual variants or disputed readings, these are noted but the critically accepted reading is translated.

## Grammatical Framework

All Sanskrit is analyzed according to Pāṇinian grammar (Aṣṭādhyāyī). Morphological parsing follows standard saṃdhi resolution, compound analysis (samāsa), and verb form identification. Lexical meanings are drawn from established Classical Sanskrit dictionaries, primarily:

- **Monier-Williams Sanskrit-English Dictionary**
- **V.S. Apte's Practical Sanskrit-English Dictionary**

## Translation Principles

### Independence from Existing Translations

This translation is produced without reference to, or alignment with, any existing English translation of the Bhagavad Gītā. The sole inputs are: the Sanskrit text, Pāṇinian grammar, established lexicons, and the internal context of the Gītā itself.

### No Imported Theological Frameworks

No sectarian or philosophical school's interpretive framework (Advaita Vedānta, Dvaita, Viśiṣṭādvaita, ISKCON devotional theology, Western philosophical categories) is imposed on the text. The translation aims to let the Sanskrit carry its own meaning without channeling it through any particular tradition's lens.

### Context-Sensitive Term Rendering

Many Sanskrit terms — *dharma*, *yoga*, *ātman*, *brahman*, *prakṛti*, *karma*, *buddhi*, among others — carry multiple valid meanings. Rather than assigning a single fixed English equivalent to each term across all occurrences, this translation selects the best-fit English rendering based on:

1. The immediate verse context
2. The surrounding passage context
3. The broader argument of the chapter

When a term is genuinely performing double duty (intentional polysemy by the author), this is noted rather than collapsed to a single meaning.

### Philosophical Density

The translation preserves the abstract and philosophically dense character of the original. Where the Sanskrit is abstract, the English remains abstract. The text is not simplified, devotionalized, or made more accessible at the cost of precision.

### Internal Tensions

Where the Gītā contains internal philosophical tensions, contradictions, or shifts in argument, these are translated faithfully and allowed to stand. The translation does not harmonize or smooth over such tensions.

## What This Translation Avoids

- **Archaisms**: No "thou," "verily," "hath," or other pseudo-biblical English unless the Sanskrit register genuinely demands elevated formality
- **Theological insertions**: Terms like "God," "Lord," "Supreme Being" are not substituted where the Sanskrit uses specific terms (*Bhagavān*, *Īśvara*, *Puruṣottama*, etc.) — these are translated or transliterated on their own merits
- **Gendered language**: Where the Sanskrit is gender-neutral, the English remains gender-neutral
- **Interpretive smoothing**: Contradictions, tensions, and difficult passages are not resolved in translation

## Translator's Notes

Notes appear only when genuinely needed:

- Flagging ambiguous terms where the contextual choice meaningfully differs from a common reading
- Recording notable textual variants in the BORI apparatus
- Identifying intentional polysemy
- Clarifying grammatical structures that affect meaning

Notes are kept concise and descriptive, not argumentative.

## Transliteration Standard

All transliteration follows the International Alphabet of Sanskrit Transliteration (IAST) standard.

### Proper Names: Diacritic vs. Anglicized Spelling

Frequently occurring proper names (e.g., *Kṛṣṇa*, *Arjuna*, *Dhṛtarāṣṭra*) are rendered at two registers:

- **Full IAST with diacritics** (*Kṛṣṇa*, *Dhṛtarāṣṭra*) is used in scholarly tiers: the literal English translation, the Devanāgarī/IAST verse block, translator's notes, and the glossary.
- **Anglicized spelling** without diacritics (*Krishna*, *Dhritarashtra*) is permitted in the readability tiers: Simple Translation, Argument Arc, Contemporary Relevance, Common Misreading, and Neuroscience Perspective sections.

Names that have no widely accepted anglicized form (e.g., *Sañjaya*, *Bhīṣma*, *Yudhiṣṭhira*) are kept in IAST throughout. The dual-register convention is for names whose anglicization is universally recognized.
