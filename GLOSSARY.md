# Glossary of Key Sanskrit Terms

This glossary tracks recurring Sanskrit terms whose English rendering varies by context throughout the translation. It is not an exhaustive Sanskrit dictionary but a guide to how key terms are handled in this translation.

Terms are listed in IAST alphabetical order.

---

### ātman
Literally "self." Depending on context, rendered as "the self," "oneself," "the inner self," or left as *ātman* when the term carries its full philosophical weight as the irreducible subject of experience.

### brahman
The absolute, the ground of being. Not translated as "God." Rendered variously as "the absolute," "brahman," or by context-specific paraphrase. Distinct from *Brahmā* (the creator deity) and *brāhmaṇa* (a member of the priestly class).

### buddhi
The faculty of discernment or judgment. Rendered as "discernment," "understanding," "judgment," or "resolve" depending on context. Not simply "intellect" — *buddhi* in the Gita often implies the capacity for decisive insight, not merely cognitive processing.

### dharma
No single English equivalent. Rendered variously as "duty," "what is right," "moral order," "law," "righteous conduct," or "the way things ought to be," depending on the immediate context. In some passages it approaches "natural constitution" or "essential function."

### guṇa
The three fundamental constituents or qualities of *prakṛti*: *sattva* (clarity/lucidity), *rajas* (activity/agitation), *tamas* (inertia/darkness). Rendered as "constituent," "quality," "strand," or left as *guṇa* depending on context.

### karma / karman
Literally "action" or "deed." Depending on context: "action," "work," "ritual action," "the results of past action," or "the causal chain of action and consequence."

### prakṛti
Material nature, the matrix of manifest existence. Rendered as "nature," "material nature," "the natural order," or left as *prakṛti* when the technical sense is paramount.

### puruṣa
Literally "person." In Sāṅkhya-inflected passages, the conscious principle as distinct from *prakṛti*. Rendered as "person," "the conscious being," "spirit," or left as *puruṣa* depending on context.

### yoga
Literally "yoking," "joining," or "discipline." Rendered variously as "discipline," "practice," "path," "method," "steadiness," or "union" depending on context. Chapter titles retain the Sanskrit *yoga*.

---

*This glossary is updated as each chapter is translated. Cross-references to specific verses are noted where a term's rendering is particularly significant or non-obvious.*
